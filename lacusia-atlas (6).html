Provincial SurveyThe Ten States & Federal District of Lacusia
Straits Federal District
Seat: Mackinac Station · Pop. ~80K · Non-voting territory
The "Lock of the Republic." Three counties commanding the Straits of Mackinac — the sole navigable passage between Lakes Michigan and Huron. Directly administered by the Federal Council under the Straits Authority Act of 1842. Home to the Lacusian Naval Headquarters, the Federal Customs Checkpoint, and the Mackinac Toll Authority, which levies transit fees on all commercial traffic passing between the two lakes. No state legislature; governed by a federally appointed Straits Commander. The bridge is both literal and symbolic — every grain barge, ore carrier, and tanker passing through this nine-mile channel pays tribute to the republic.
Federal Territory Naval HQ Toll Authority Customs Checkpoint
Superior
Capital: Baawiting (Sault Ste. Marie) · Pop. 2.5M · GDP: $180B
The iron heart of the republic, wrapping the entire southern and western shore of Lake Superior — from the Arrowhead of Minnesota through the Michigan Upper Peninsula to the Ontario north shore. Baawiting takes its name from the Ojibwe for "place of the rapids," honoring the St. Marys River falls that defined trade here for ten thousand years. Copper and iron ore from the Mesabi and Gogebic ranges underpin the Lacusian Iron Reserve. The Superior Ore Docks at Baawiting are the largest freshwater port facilities on earth. Boreal forest, Anishinaabe treaty lands, and the Pictured Rocks coastline define a state with fierce pride in its isolation and its indispensability.
Iron & Copper Mining Boreal Timber Superior Shore
Anishinaabe
Capital: Animikiiwing (Thunder Bay) · Pop. 0.8M · GDP: $60B
The northernmost Lacusian state and the one most explicitly shaped by Indigenous sovereignty. Animikiiwing — "place of the Thunderbird" — was founded as a co-governance territory under the 1836 Charter's Indigenous Partner provisions, and remains the only state with a bicameral legislature that requires equal representation from the Anishinaabe Confederacy and the general electorate. The Thunder Bay and Kenora districts of Ontario, combined with the far northeastern Minnesota Arrowhead, form a vast boreal corridor draining toward Hudson Bay. Chrome, gold, and rare earth mineral rights are administered jointly with the Nishnawbe Aski Nation. The least populous state; the most sovereign.
Indigenous Sovereignty Rare Earth Minerals Hudson Bay Watershed
Michigania
Capital: Lakeshire (Chicago) · Pop. 13M · GDP: $1.5T
The economic engine of the republic. Lakeshire commands the southern tip of Lake Michigan and functions as the financial, cultural, and logistical capital of Lacusia in all but name — the actual national capital, Lac-Maumee, is smaller and more symbolic. The Lacusian Board of Grain, the largest commodity exchange on earth, operates from the Lakeshire Mercantile Tower. The state absorbs the former Illinois lakefront, southeastern Wisconsin (including Milwald), and the northern Indiana dune coast — a megalopolis stretching 120 miles along the lake. Architecture, media, cuisine, and finance radiate outward from here. Michigania is the republic's loudest argument for its own greatness.
Finance & Grain Exchange Manufacturing Lake Michigan Arc
Huronia
Capital: Mnidoo-Mnis (Manitoulin Island) · Pop. 2.2M · GDP: $140B
Named for the Wendat (Huron) confederacy who dominated this freshwater maritime world before European contact, Huronia covers the northern Lower Michigan peninsula and the great Canadian inland sea of Georgian Bay — including Manitoulin Island, the largest freshwater island on earth. The capital, Mnidoo-Mnis ("Spirit Island" in Ojibwe), sits on the island's eastern shore and governs via ferry network rather than road. The state has no interstate highway; it has shipping lanes. Commercial fisheries, artisan boat-building, French-Métis culture, and a growing ecotourism economy define a state that has always looked outward across water rather than inward to land.
Georgian Bay Maritime Freshwater Fisheries Métis Heritage
Wyandot-Huron
Capital: Détroit · Pop. 8M · GDP: $680B
The republic's industrial twin-city corridor, built on the narrowest water crossing in the entire Great Lakes system. Détroit and its mirror city Windsor were never separated in Lacusia — the river that was once an international border is now a municipal waterway, and the two cities share a transit system, a school curriculum, and a mayor's council. The southern Lower Michigan peninsula and the Essex County Ontario shoreline form one contiguous metropolitan industrial zone. Assembly plants, advanced battery manufacturing, pharmaceutical research corridors, and the Federal Institute of Engineering make Wyandot-Huron the republic's most productive state per capita. The Wendat name is honored; the French name is kept.
Auto & Advanced Mfg. Pharma & R&D Detroit River Corridor
Ontaria
Capital: Tronto · Pop. 14M · GDP: $1.1T
The eastern counterweight to Michigania. Tronto — the Lacusian rendering of the Mohawk word "tkaronto" ("where there are trees standing in the water") — anchors a megaregion stretching from Hamilton and Niagara through Kingston to Ottawa and the upper St. Lawrence. With 14 million people, Ontaria is the republic's most populous state and its second-largest economy, trailing only Michigania. It is also the republic's most polyglot: forty languages are spoken in Tronto's Regent Park district alone. The Niagara peninsula produces the republic's finest wines; the Ottawa corridor produces its finest bureaucrats. Ontaria controls the eastern gateway to the St. Lawrence Seaway and levies its own port fees at Tronto Harbor, a perennial source of intergovernmental tension.
Finance & Tech St. Lawrence Gateway Niagara Viticulture
Ontara
Capital: Érieton (Cleveland) · Pop. 4.5M · GDP: $380B
The rust-belt state that refused to rust. Érieton sits at the mouth of the Cuyahoga River — a waterway so famously combustible that its 1969 fire became a founding myth of the Water Commons Doctrine. Today the Cuyahoga is a kayaking corridor and the Flats district is a biomedical research campus. Ontara's Mahoning Valley still runs steel, but cleaner and smarter; the Akron polymer corridor leads the republic in advanced materials; the Cleveland Clinic is the nation's largest single healthcare employer. The state is bounded on the north by Lake Erie and on the east by the Pennsylvania border, which Lacusia has never formally recognized as meaningful — a point of ongoing diplomatic complexity.
Steel & Polymers Healthcare & Biotech Erie Shore
Érié
Capital: Lac-Maumee (Toledo) · Pop. 3.5M · GDP: $240B
The national capital state. Lac-Maumee was chosen as capital in 1836 precisely because it was nobody's first choice — equidistant between Lakeshire's commercial ambitions and Érieton's industrial pride, rooted in the Black Swamp drainage basin that had defined the region's agricultural character for millennia. The Maumee River runs through the capital to Lake Erie; the Wabash-Erie Canal corridor runs south to Algonquin. The Federal Council chambers sit on the river's north bank. Érié state wraps the southwestern Erie shore — island wine country, commercial fishing harbors, the Lacusian Navy's Kelleys Island station — and extends south through the rich Ohio agricultural interior. The most politically centrist state; the symbolic heart of the republic.
National Capital Black Swamp Agriculture Erie Maritime
Algonquin
Capital: Kekionga (Fort Wayne) · Pop. 5M · GDP: $280B
Named for the great Algonquin language family whose speakers held every portage in this territory before European contact. Kekionga — the Miami-Illinois name for the confluence of the St. Marys, St. Joseph, and Maumee rivers at what is now Fort Wayne — is the republic's most inland capital, and the most deliberate act of historical reckoning in the founding geography. The Maumee-Wabash portage, a half-mile overland carry that connected the Great Lakes to the Mississippi basin, is now a federal heritage corridor. Soybean and corn production from the Indiana and Ohio interiors are foundational to Lacusia's agricultural export economy. The state also hosts the Central Rail Junction, where the Erie Spine meets the North-South Gateway — the republic's most critical infrastructure interchange.
Corn, Soy & Grain Rail Junction Indigenous Heritage

IV
Commerce & TransportStrategic Trade Routes
Straits Federal Passage The Lock of the Republic
Every vessel transiting between Lakes Michigan and Huron passes through the nine-mile Straits of Mackinac under the jurisdiction of the Straits Federal District. The Toll Authority collects transit fees scaled by cargo tonnage and vessel class. Iron ore, grain, petroleum, and manufactured goods all pay tribute here. The revenue funds the Lacusian Navy, the federal infrastructure endowment, and the Water Commons Authority. No cargo moves north or south between the two great mid-lakes without Lacusia's permission and its price.
$4.2Bannual toll revenue
Erie Rail Spine Transcontinental Freight Corridor
The republic's primary east-west freight artery, running 800 miles along the Erie shore from Érieton through Lac-Maumee, Détroit, and Lakeshire. Eighteen daily express passenger trains; continuous bulk freight. The spine connects Ontaria's St. Lawrence gateway to Michigania's grain exchange, threading through every major population center along the southern lake shore. It is the circulatory system of Lacusian commerce — and the line that, if severed, would stop the republic cold.
$380Bannual freight flow
St. Lawrence Seaway Corridor Atlantic Export Gateway
Lacusia's outlet to world markets. Bulk carriers laden with iron ore, grain, and manufactured goods pass eastward through Lake Ontario, the Thousand Islands, and the St. Lawrence toward Montreal and the Atlantic. Ontaria's port at Tronto Harbor is the republic's largest seagoing terminal; the federal government maintains treaty rights over the western seaway locks. The corridor is the subject of ongoing negotiation with the Atlantic Republics, who resent Lacusian leverage over cargo heading to their ports.
$520Bannual flow
Superior Ore Circuit Northern Iron Route
Raw iron ore from the Mesabi Range and Gogebic Iron Range moves by rail to the Superior Ore Docks at Baawiting, then by laker vessel eastward through the Soo Locks (administered by the Straits Federal District as a subsidiary authority), across Lake Huron, and south to the steel mills of Wyandot-Huron and Ontara. This circuit — mine to mill in 72 hours — is the backbone of Lacusian heavy industry and the reason Superior's relative poverty belies its strategic indispensability.
$210Bannual flow
Kekionga Portage Corridor Continental Interior Gateway
The ancient Maumee-Wabash portage — a half-mile overland carry between two river systems — now carries fiber-optic trunk lines, natural gas pipelines, and the North-South Gateway rail line connecting Michigania to the Republic of the Mississippi Valley. Lacusia maintains sovereign treaty rights over this corridor under the 1836 Charter. It is the single most sensitive point in Lacusia's relationship with its southern neighbors, who view the portage tolls as extortion and Lacusia views as existential infrastructure security.
$145Bannual flow
Tronto–Lakeshire Air Bridge Domestic Aviation Spine
The busiest domestic air corridor in the republic, running between O'Lacus International (Lakeshire) and Tronto Pearson Federal (Ontaria). Hourly departures; 22 million passengers annually on this route alone. The two cities are economic rivals, cultural counterweights, and — in the republic's founding mythology — the "twin poles" around which Lacusian identity revolves. Lakeshire is money and ambition; Tronto is refinement and diversity. Neither would admit the other is indispensable. Both are.

V
Civic StructureGovernment & Constitution
⚖️

Federal Council

A bicameral legislature: the Assembly of Waters (lower, proportional by population) and the Senate of States (upper, 4 senators per state). Capital sits at Lake Lacus. Sessions begin at the spring thaw — a constitutional tradition.

🏛️

Executive — The Wardenship

The Warden of Lacusia is elected by popular vote for 5-year terms, max two. The Warden commands the Lacusian Fleet, signs trade treaties, and may veto legislation with a three-fifths council override. Current Warden: Elara Mossbridge (Ontara). The office is symbolically inaugurated at Mackinac Station, in the Straits Federal District — no Warden may govern who has not stood at the Lock of the Republic.

🌊

Water Commons Doctrine

The founding constitutional principle: the Great Lakes are held in common trust for all citizens. No private ownership of shoreline, no water export without unanimous Council approval. The most litigated clause in Lacusian constitutional history.

🏭

Strategic Reserves

Lacusia maintains the Iron & Steel Reserve (Superior), the Grain Strategic Buffer (Algonquin), the Rare Earth Mineral Compact (Anishinaabe), and the Freshwater Sovereignty Fund — a sovereign wealth fund built on water-use licensing fees paid by neighboring republics, now exceeding $270B annually.

⚓

Lacusian Navy & Coast Guard

A freshwater navy operating on all five lakes, headquartered at Mackinac Station in the Straits Federal District. Primary mission: toll enforcement, commerce protection, environmental interdiction, and border patrol. The Kelleys Island Naval Station in Érié houses the fleet's second command; the flagship LRS Black Swamp is permanently berthed at the Straits.

📜

The Charter of 1836

Signed at the Battle of the Maumee Rapids, the founding document recognizes Indigenous nations as sovereign treaty partners, establishes the Straits Federal District as a permanent federal territory, guarantees water commons across all five lakes, and enshrines the right to navigate all interior waterways. The ten states and one federal district are bound by the Water Commons Doctrine — often called "the wettest constitution in the world."
